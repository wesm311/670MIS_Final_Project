Sentiment and Skepticism in tweets referencing global warming¶

Wesley Matson¶

MIS 670¶

Climate change is an increasingly polarizing topic. In recent history world temperatures have slowly been increasing. Over the last ten years, the pace of increase is accelerating. The scientific community is united behind the idea that manmade impact is causing the recent increase in temperatures. Scientist point to the raise in greenhouse gasses as the biggest contributor to higher temperatures. Greenhouses gasses allow sunlight to pass through into earth, but traps of the energy that normally is radiated back out to space. Evidence of damage to the earth can be seen by warming oceans, shrinking ice sheets, glacial retreat, decreased snow cover and sea level rise. If the current trends go unchecked, the artic will become ice free in the summer, sea level will rise 1-4 ft, hurricanes will become stronger, drought and head waves will be more extreme and precipitation patterns will continue to change.
Despite the scientific evidence, some remain skeptical man has any influence on the weather patterns. Even though the amount of carbon dioxide in the air has increased drastically over the past 50 years, it still accounts for only 0.04% of the atmosphere. Skeptics use this to argue that it cannot have a large effect on global temperatures because water vapor is also a greenhouse gas and makes up a much higher proportion of the atmosphere. Skeptics also point to the last couple years being slightly cooler than before to point to a new trend of global cooling. Another theory include heightened sun activity caused the recent run up in temperatures and temperatures will start to cool when sun activity decreases. If climate change is indeed manmade, the time to change our behavior and combat climate change is now. The longer we wait, the direr the situation becomes. Currently there is no political will to enact the policies and laws that are required to combat climate change. The upcoming presidential election will have a great impact on if the government will start to take action to mitigate possible climate changing behavior. The main goal of this project is to analyze twitter data for present time (April 2019), April 2015, and April 2011 to see if there are any significant differences between the sentiment and skepticism of tweets in these time periods. Any changes in the present time may signal a shift in voter sentiment with respect to global warming.

Data Collection¶

Data for current tweets will be collected via twitter API with mentions of 'global warming' or 'climate change'. Data was collected from April 17 through April 19 2019. Overall, 376007 tweets where collected for further processing. To get the data for past tweets, python package GOT was used. 100 tweets where collected from April 2015 and April 2011. These are the times that are the most similiar in terms of presidential election cycle.

Skepticism Analasis via text classification¶

Skepticism is a difficult challange when analizing tweets mentioning 'climate change'. Topic modeling will not seperate the tweets, the difference is subtle. It is possible to use text classification, but the tweets will have to be manually labeled before feeding into the algorithm. Then, we can label use the prediction to label the rest of the tweets. There are many tweets that do not explicitly support or are skeptical of 'climate change'. For simplicity, we have grouped these with supporting tweets, and attached labels of skeptical (0) and not skeptical (1)

Conclusion¶

Monitoring twitter and other social media streams is an important way to keep up with the ever-changing issues that motivate voters. From the analysis, we can see that despite sentiment of ‘climate change’ tweets holding steady, the skepticism expressed has increased over the past 8 years. This implies that voters are less likely to accept the status quo when it comes to climate change, and voters are ready for action, one way or the other.
